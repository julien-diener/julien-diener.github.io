I have been coding since I am a kid. After a B.Sc. in Math at Heriot-Watt University (Edinburgh), I got a Ph.D. in Computer Graphics at INPG (Grenoble). I then worked in several projects, with physicists, biologists, and now with quants (the math part of finance).

So, on one hand, I think I am a good coder with strong math skill. And I consider my-self a Full-Stack Hacker:

  • Full-Stack because I make decisions based on all involved components and efficiency of the development process
  • Hacker because I like to find and take advantages from the subtleties-that-matter

And on the other hand, during my career working with many different people and topics, I became particularly proficient for the design of numerical workflow in complex, interdisciplinary, projects. Also, I developed a strong managerial sense based on two main principles:

  • Honesty: to put it simply, the expectation of all collaborators, and clients, should be clear
  • Short cycles and agility: every communication, development and test steps should be as short, frequent and automatized as possible. Agile methods are here to help us.
